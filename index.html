Introduction

Shivaji Maharaj (1630-1680) was the founder of the Maratha Empire and a visionary leader who fought against the Mughal Empire and other foreign powers to establish Hindavi Swarajya (self-rule for Hindus). Known for his military genius, especially in guerrilla warfare, he built a powerful navy and captured key forts across western India. His reign emphasized good governance, religious tolerance, and efficient administration. Early Life

Shivaji Maharaj was born in the hill-fort of Shivneri, located near Pune, Maharashtra. His father, Shahaji Bhonsle, was a Maratha general who served the Bijapur Sultanate, while his mother, Jijabai, deeply influenced his upbringing. Shivaji was brought up with a strong sense of Hindu culture and values, which later shaped his vision of establishing a free and sovereign Maratha state.

Vision and Values

Shivaji Maharaj was known for his strong vision to establish a Hindavi Swarajya (self-rule of Hindus), a kingdom free from the oppression of foreign powers like the Mughals and Bijapur Sultanate. He was deeply influenced by his mother Jijabai and the teachings of saints such as Samarth Ramdas. His values centered on protecting his people, promoting justice, and ensuring freedom of religion.

Administration and Governance

Shivaji Maharaj established an efficient and well-structured administration. He divided his kingdom into provinces, each governed by trusted officials. His Ashtapradhan (Council of Eight Ministers) helped him in governing various aspects of the kingdom, such as military, finance, justice, and foreign relations.

Some of his key administrative reforms:

  • Taxation System: He abolished oppressive taxes like the jagirdari system and instead collected taxes directly from the peasants. The main taxes were Chauth (25% of the revenue) and Sardeshmukhi (10% extra tax), ensuring a stable income for the kingdom.
  • Navy: Shivaji built one of the first naval forces in India to protect the coastline from foreign invasions, particularly from the Portuguese and the British.
  • Religious Tolerance: Though Shivaji was a devout Hindu, he respected all religions and appointed Muslims to important positions in his army and administration. He ensured that his soldiers respected women and places of worship during his military campaigns.
